The room was very nice and the bed was comfortable. We were on the 4th floor and had a great view of the Eiffel tower. I like the small, 2 person, elevator but it would be claustrophobic to some. No free breakfast. We were at the corner of the Rue Cler which is a less touristy part of Paris and close to the tower. The service of the staff was good as expected however, they were not overly friendly as compared to other hotel staff in other counties we stayed at during our tour of Europe. They didn't interact with us much, such as recommending places to eat at or visit. The staff did say we could leave our backpacks there the day of checkout when we asked.
We stayed in this hotel for three nights in June 2011, and though that it was great. The room we had overlooked the street (which is pedestrianised) but the double glazing prevented this being a problem. The room was small but had everything that we needed, including a bathroom which was recently refurbished. We had breakfast in the hotel breakfast room on the last morning we were there, and it was excellent value. There are two brasseries on either side of the street just outside the hotel, and shops and a supermarket (plus of course lots more restaurants and cafes) in the vicinity. It is about 6 minutes walk to the nearest Metro station. All in all, a good place to stay.

We visited Paris for the first time in June 2010 and booked the Hotel du Champ De Mars . The hotel was recommended by several known guide books. We feel very fortunate to have found such a nice hotel. The staff were very friendly and helpful. Rooms were very spacious (compared to the other ones we stayed in Europe), clean and quaint. The neighbourhood the hotel is located in (Rue Cler) is fantastic. Great little shops and cafes. We thoroughly enjoyed our stay at the hotel and would stay there again. We have already recommended the hotel to several people who will be visiting Paris soon.
