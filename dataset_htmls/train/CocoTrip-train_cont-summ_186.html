On my first trip to Turkey, a friend and I decided to go to Istanbul a few days early and found the Hotel Nena website. We had a great time. The hotel offers airport pickup with a driver who is quite above the average taxi you might take from the airport. The rooms were very comfortable and the food served at the hotel was delicious. Many hotels in Istanbul offer poor breakfasts of Tang and tired food. (We had this at the group hotel.) The Hotel Nena offered fresh orange juice, strawberries, melon and other healthy and fresh food. The restaurant terrace is above the Blue Mosque, the hotel is convenient to the tram line, there is a lovely garden area to relax and the personnel at the hotel were helpful and friendly.
We stayed at the Nena Hotel for 5 nights. We upgraded to a deluxe room (#401), and we were very happy with our choice. We had a lovely view of the Blue Mosque and also the bridge crossing to the modern side of Istanbul. We had heard from others that the standard rooms are small; for five nights the larger room was great. We LOVED staying in the old part of Istanbul, and the Nena Hotel was perfectly located for exploring so many parts of that area. The staff at the Nena Hotel were friendly and quite helpful. The breakfast buffet was very plentiful and the view from the restaurant terrace was very nice.
The Nena Hotel was excellent. The hotel is close to all of the major sites in the historic part of Istanbul. A SHORT walk to the Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, Topkapi Palace, Basilica Cistern, and the Grand Bazaar. A slightly longer walk will take you to the shore to enjoy the sea and the restaurants that line the piers. The tram to Taksim Square - the trendy, hipster hot spot - is close to the hotel. The hotel is well decorated and comfortable. The room and the bathroom were very nice. The hotel staff was helpful and friendly. The breakfast was enjoyable.
We were recently, May 17 -20th, at the Nena Hotel. The service was excellent and the accommodations were very comfortable. We intend to go back to Istanbul and we would certainly stay at the Nena Hotel, without a doubt.
