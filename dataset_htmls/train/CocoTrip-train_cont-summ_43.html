Lovely Hotel. This hotel is a very very nice hotel. I booked this through the hotel website (something I don't normally do, as I usually use one of the last-minute discount hotel websites) and stayed for four nights.The hotel is very new and everything is impeccably clean. It is very central, with a short walk to Ginza and the JR line a 4 minutes walk and two other lines at the side door of the hotel. The lobby is on the 25th floor and the rooms are above, with an atrium in the middle.The room is small (average by Asian room standards), but not squashed. The price is less than a comparable room in, say, Hong Kong, Sydney, New York or Paris and the hotel staff are perfect.

Great Location with Awesome Skyline View of Tokyo Park Hotel is in a great location of Ginza district . It is walking distance to the posh shopping district in Ginza, as well as the to the famous Fish market. It is also about 7 minute walk to the JR Shimbashi station on the Yamonote, which can get you to all of the tourist attractions in Tokyo. The hotel room is a quite roomy compared to the business hotels in Japan with awesome views of the city. The hotel also has a fantastic lobby with live jazz music. For the price that we paid (about $160/night) this hotel seems like the biggest bang for buck in Tokyo. I also recommend the restaurants on the first floor or basement for cheap lunches.

Summary

Lovely hotel. The Park Hotel is a very hip hotel for a very good price. The lobby is impressive, as it is an open space that rises from the 25th floor, where the registration desk is, to the 35th floor. The morning breakfast buffet, which is served in the lobby, was good. The Japanese restaurant in the hotel was excellent. As for the room, the decor was nice, and the bed was comfortable, although a bit firm. The bathroom was small, but adequate. The location of the hotel is within walking distance of the Ginza shopping district and the fish market. It also sits above the subway station. The only downside to the hotel was the ventilation system, which filled with cigarette smoke from the other rooms.
