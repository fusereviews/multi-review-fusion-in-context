Great old hotel in a great location I stayed at the Ambassador a week ago and had a wonderful time! I did have one of the infamous windowless rooms (the hotel is an old renovated warehouse of sorts) but the ambiance of the authentic New Orleans decor (exposed brick, high ceilings, wood floors) made it almost seem like being in old France. The hotel is located close to everything there is in New Orleans and a close stumble to Bourbon St. I found the valet staff to be prompt and friendly even though they were busy and the front desk staff was quick to attend to any request with a smile. I am definetly staying at this hotel next time I am in New Orleans, and I am curious to see how wonderful the renovations will turn out!
Great spot, great rate, great effort! Just returned from stay for French Quarter Fest. As we arrived, a bus was dropping off a full load of European students to stay at the hotel. Despite the sudden rush, we were checked in cheerfully and quickly. Everyone went out of their way. Location is great for getting to (or away from) Quarter craziness or Garden District, and Warehouse area has its own charm and eateries. Room was comfortable and quiet, if a bit dark. Bandito's off the lobby great for delicious breakfast with no wait (!) or tequila any time. We'd recommend the Ambassador to anyone traveling to NoLa for biz or pleasure.

Summary

The hotel is an old historical building but with genuine decor befitting New Orleans. Very authentic. This hotel is located to everything you need in New Orleans and not far from Bourbon Street. The rooms were of an average quality, with rather basic furniture and facilities. The cleanliness of the rooms aren't to a very high standard either. The stay started off well because the valet staff are efficient and despite being really busy they maintained a friendly attitude. The front desk staff were also attentive and lovely, but the management let the staff down because they were clearly disinterested in tending to customers. The breakfast options were really limited to pastries and some coffee. Despite inexpensive rates and free parking this hotel is potentially dangerous; there are bed bug infestations that can leave you with huge itching welts.
