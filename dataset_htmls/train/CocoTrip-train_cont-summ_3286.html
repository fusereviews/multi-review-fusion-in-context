stayed at this hotel for three nights. It is very central to most of the tourist sites in Rome but on a very busy, noisy street. Sound proofing was not very good and even with the windows closed, the rooms facing the street were very noisy long into the night. We were given a new updated room which was very comfortable and modern with a flat screen TV, however the new bathroom did not have a shower screen which meant the floor was flooded after every shower with only two towels and no bathmat. The best was the breakfast in a lovely glassed enclosed verandah on the 5th floor, plenty to satisfy all tastes. Don't let me put you off completely but I have stayed in better hotels in Rome for less.
I absolutely enjoyed the quantness of this small hotel, situated around the corner from the Trevi Fountain. I found it very centrally situated as we were in walking distance to all the major spots. The Concierge/Receptionist was always very helpful and pleasant. It has an old fashioned tiny lift that just fits two people but it is beautiful. The room we were in was suitably sized, and although on a busy main road, the windows were double glazed and blocked out all noise, and we slept peacefully. The bathroom was lovely with a huge shower. Breakfast was excellent. We stayed at a few other hotel during our trip to Italy but none had the particular charm that the Tritone had. I definately would stay there again
I have just come back from staying with some friends at the Tritone Hotel. All facilities were very modern in the room, and the hotel was in an ideal location for sightseeing. Breakfast was available until 10.30 which was nice if you don't want to get up too early, it was set up in a rooftop breakfast room, with a view over the city. Our flight didnt leave until late on the last day, and we were able to leave our bags at reception whilst we did shopping which was very handy. The room was a bit noisy in the morning due to the fact that the hotel is on a main road, but it is double glazed and you are in the heart of the city.
It was obvious the room (101) was recently renovated, it was very clean, comfortable and fully equiped, although a bit small. The location was fantastic, very close to Trevi Fountain, Via Condotti and Piazza Montecitorio, that is in walking distance of everything that must be seen in Rome's historic center. The breakfast room was at the last floor of the building and had a beautiful roof-top view. The staff were very helpful and spoke fluent English. What i liked least, was the noise- it was annoying coming both from inside the hotel (people speaking in the corridors) as well as outside (traffic from the street) and it was annoying, especially in the night.
