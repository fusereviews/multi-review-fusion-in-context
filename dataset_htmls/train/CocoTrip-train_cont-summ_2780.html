Just stayed at the ESH after spending a 3 day trip in Rome. The hotel is rather nice and modern, very clean. The staff are not exactly the friendliest bunch of people in the world especially when you ask for a drink at the bar (which is hardly ever attended by staff). The hotel is situated in the student area of Rome alongside universities, it is NOT central but has a bus stop outside. Its a 20 minute walk to the nearest underground metro station. The real joke came when I took 2 cokes out of the mini bar after reading on the mini bar menu they were 3 euros each but when i came to checkout they slapped an 18 percent city tax on 2 cokes! Making them the princely sum of 24 euros! Outrageous!
The hotel is a converted monastery which has been open for about a year. The refurbishment has been carried out to a high standard, and as you would expect in Italy, is both elegant and stylish. The colours throughout the building are pale cream through to chocolate brown and all shades in between - delightful. Our room was spacious and the bathroom fabulous. The staff, led by the owner, were helpful and pleasant. The bottle of wine on arrival was a welcome gesture. A decent breakfast was provided and I can only describe the food in the restaurant as superb, particularly the fish dishes. It is a shame that the hotel did not sell tickets for the buses but they are prevented from doing so by the City of Rome authorities.

Summary

Travelled to the ESH Hotel, it was very reasonably priced if a little out of the way. The Rome bus service is very hit and miss so travelled on the Metro most of the time. The ESH is very modern and very well presented, the rooms are spacious however no tea/coffee facilities in the room and no English channels on the TV not even world news. The breakfast is continental style and plentiful with a good coffee machine with loads of choices. We were B&B, and we did find a really good supermarket up the street for nibbles in the room. We only had one drink in the bar due to the top prices charged so beware.
