We stayed at the Waldorf 3rd - 7th July 2005. We were given a very large corner room on the 14th floor and it was just what we'd expected of the Waldorf. The decor in the room was tasteful and in keeping with the rest of the hotel (the rest of the hotel being very plush from what we saw!) and the bed, although not the biggest, was the most comfy we had ever stayed in! On our first night they also bought us a bottle of champagne and strawberrys dipped in chocolate because we were on our honeymoon! You can't beat the location of the hotel either, it's in walking distance to just about everything. Overall an extremely pleasant stay and we would definitely recommend it to others.
This is the second time we've stayed at the Waldorf, the first time we were upgraded to a fantastic suite, this time we were given the smallest hotel room I've ever seen, for three of us! We asked to move and the room we were given was certainly larger but with peeling wallpaper, ripped cushions on the furniture and a very unusual smell. We had originally booked for four nights but checked out the next day and went to the London Hotel on 54th and 7th which was a breath of fresh air. The Waldorf has great history and a rare presence, but if you need quality accommodation at a this price you can do a lot better. There is no wireless internet in the room and the wired service is expensive.
This hotel is amazing. From the moment we checked in, we felt like Royalty. We checked in early, and as expected our room wasn't ready. Rather than tell us to just check our bags, we were given access to the elegant lounge on the 26th floor to have a refreshment and a snack. It was amazing. We set out for the day after checking our bags only to return to the most incredible room I have ever seen-and I've seen a lot of rooms. We were upgraded to an Astor Suite! Basically a beautiful room with a sitting area. Not enough room to say all the good things about this facility, but if you ever have the chance-stay here. I can promise you won't regret it.
We were booked to stay here 9-13 November 2011 and it was amazing. Rooms were very comfortable and clean - as we would expect. The beds have to be the most comfortable we have ever experienced in a hotel. Our room was by the lifts on the 15th floor (not that we heard them) and although we didn't have a view this did not spoil our stay. The service was superb and the food choice for breakfast was unbeliveable. Prices were as expected for a hotel of this calibre although we didn't touch the mini bar as felt this was quite pricey! Well placed to explore this fabulous city and we were very lucky with the weather. Would we stay here again? - you bet we would!
Our first time in New York City and we wanted it to be memorable. Our suite was elegant and everything we expected. Just walking into the hotel was a treat and we were thrilled when we were shown our room. The hotel lives up to its past glory and is a gem. We found the location to be within walking distance to may of our planned sightseeing spots and Grand Central Station if we needed the subway. Taxis were also always in que as well. We did not dine at the hotel as prices were said to be quite high. We were very happy we chose to stay at the Waldorf, well worth the price for the experience of a bygone era of elegance.

Summary

We found the Waldorf Astoria to be a very pleasant place to stay. The rooms are clean and the beds are very comfortable. The staff are friendly and helpful. The hotel is located on Park Avenue and Lexington, Its close to the subway which gives access to the whole of New York and it's easily accessible to Times Square and the other sites in New York.
