The strongest point of this hotel is its location. The Metro station was just around the corner and proved to be very convenient for our daily commute. The double room of the hotel had a small balcony overlooking a park. The room was small and the rates were a tad expensive for its size. The bathroom had glazed glass doors, without any lock, and was small as well. Nevertheless, the room was well maintained and clean. The breakfast spread was also good. The most negative point of this hotel is its elevator - the smallest I have ever seen - just 2 people and it is full ! It stopped working when we were checking out and we had to carry all our luggage through the stairs.

Summary

The hotel is a 3 star but in the centre of Paris and within easy distance of most attractions and very close to an underground station. The rooms are quite small but clean and the staff are very helpful. One issue would be that the walls are very thin and you can hear the televisions of adjoining rooms and even the occupants talking. Also the air con doesn't work sometimes in the rooms.
