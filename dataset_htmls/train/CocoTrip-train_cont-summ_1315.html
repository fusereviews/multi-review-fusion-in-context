After browsing through a long list of hotels in Rome, I decided on staying here, as Alessandrino appeared to have the best price-quality ratio. And so it did. Although located on a pretty steep hill, it's closer to the center than many others in its price range. The Vatican is no more than 15 minutes away by foot. The rooms are.huge. The one we stayed in even had an interesting curved shape, so you didn't feel you were living in a box. The only downside was the poor breakfast. Admittedly, during the summer you can have it on the terrace atop the hotel, from where you can see far into the distance, to the mountains, and closer to the Vatican dome. Would definitely recommend this hotel and would stay there again.
It was our first trip to Rome and we (two couples) stayed the week before Christmas for 3 nights and we can't fault it! The location was ideal - nice and quiet residential area close to the Vatican. The hotel was very clean and nicely decorated. The staff were all very pleasant and helpful and we would love to go back again! We looked at the reviews and it was helpful to know the hotel was situated on top of a hill. Yes, there are steps up to it, alternatively there is a gently sloping pathway - the choice is yours. But get a taxi back if you can't manage either of these - it really is not a problem! Rome itself is abolutely wonderful and the people so friendly. Enjoy. Ciao!
