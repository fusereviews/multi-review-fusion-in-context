This was my second time in Venice and I learnt the hard way that it is a good idea to pick a hotel near to the train station if you have luggage! This hotel meets this requirement. The hotel is cheap for Venetian standards but the value for money is not so good compared to other cities. A full breakfast was advertised when I booked. What was provided was very far from that - two bread rolls, jam, coffee and juice. The room was small but okay. The shared bathroom was not so good. Poor water pressure and not much of a barrier between the shower and the rest of the room. In terms of location I would recommend this hotel however some simple adjustments need to be made to service and maintenance before recommending overall.

Hotel Rossi is a 'budget hotel' by Venice standards. I did a lot of research before booking this Hotel and this came recommended from a friend who had stayed there a year before. The room was small but big enough. Could have been bigger if it did not have so much unnecessary furniture. It was very quiet. We choose a ground floor room because we were travelling with a baby and did not want to carry a pram and luggage upstairs. The breakfast was basic but fine and a good way to start the day. The woman who served the breakfast was very very nice. The front desk staff were a bit cool but ok. The Hotel is very conveniently located. I would recommend to others travelling to Venice.
We stayed at hotel Rossi with our 6 month year old baby. The hotel is a great location but a very average hotel. We paid 112 euros for a double room. The hotel has no lift so we had to carry the pram up three sets of stairs. We asked the hotel if we could get some boiling water for our babies formula, the receptionist took us to the coffee machine, showed us it was off and said we couldn't use it until 7.30am the next morning and that the room was locked. We found the staff particularly unfriendly and unhelpful. The rooms are very very basic but clean. The breakfast is very basic, 4 bread rolls and jam and coffee.
We were passing through by car so this hotel worked out very well in that the parking was easy and free, yet the location was still close to town and the train station. The walk into town was about 15-20 minutes, or the hotel has bikes that you can use. The bikes were all checked out when we arrived so if you know you want to use them it is best to reserve them in advance. The room wasn't anything fancy but the hotel was clean and acceptable for a 2 or 3 star type hotel.

Summary

This place is good value for a very expensive city. It's conveniently located near the train stations. Rooms are small but comfortable and clean, and the bathrooms are tiny, but clean and usable. Since the hotel is at the end of a small alley, it's fairly quiet. The breakfast is very basic, but the coffee is good and the rolls are fresh. If you're looking for a place to comfortably sleep and shower, then this hotel is great.
